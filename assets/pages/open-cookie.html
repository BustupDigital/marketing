Cookie Policy

1. What Are Cookies?

By using the Site or any of our online services, you agree that we may place one or more cookies—small text files with unique identifier codes—on your device. We utilize session cookies, which expire when your browser closes, and persistent cookies, which remain until they expire or are manually removed. For instructions on managing or disabling cookies, please refer to your browser’s “Help” resources. Note that blocking or deleting cookies may restrict your access to certain features of Hola Marketing products and services. We may also employ third-party cookies (e.g., Google Analytics—see https://www.google.com/policies/privacy/partners for more information).

By accepting cookies, you permit us to automatically gather technical data from your device through various methods, including “web beacons” or “clear GIFs” (together with cookies, “cookies and similar technologies”). This data may include your IP address, device identifiers, browser and operating system details, pages viewed immediately before and after visiting our Site, content interactions, and timestamps. We may also monitor interactions with our emails (e.g., opens, clicks, and forwards).

2. Cookies Used by Hola Marketing

We utilize several categories of cookies to provide and enhance our products and services:

Strictly Necessary Cookies: Required to deliver the services you request, such as securing communications and preventing attacks. Disabling these cookies will prevent you from using our services.

Preference (Functionality) Cookies: Remember your selections (language, region, login details) and distribute server load to improve performance and response times.

Statistics (Performance) Cookies: Collect anonymous data on how you use our services (pages viewed, links clicked) to help us improve the user experience.

Marketing Cookies: Track your online behavior to deliver relevant advertisements and limit the number of times the same ad is shown.

4. Cookie Duration

Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain until they expire (typically between 30 minutes and two years) or are deleted. You can manage or disable cookies via your browser settings (usually under “Tools” or “Preferences”). Blocking cookies may impact certain site functions. For detailed instructions, consult your browser’s help documentation or visit https://www.allaboutcookies.org.

5. “Do Not Track” Signals

Since there is no consistent industry standard for honoring “Do Not Track” signals, we do not modify our data collection practices when such signals are received.

We may update this Policy periodically. Revised versions will be posted on our Site with the date of the latest revision. We will make reasonable efforts to notify you of any material changes.
